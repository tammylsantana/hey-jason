# Founder — Tammy Santana

**BTS Innovations, Inc.**

---

## The $3,600 Lesson

Before Wizard of Apps existed, Tammy spent $3,600 across multiple AI platforms trying to build apps. She never shipped a single one.

**What went wrong:**

- Used GPT-4 for precision tasks — it confidently gave her code that referenced packages that don't exist
- Used Claude for quick fixes — got a 500-word essay when she needed 5 lines
- Didn't know Gemini was better for research, or that Replit Agent could actually run and test code
- Wrote vague prompts and burned through API credits getting unusable responses
- Had no way to know if her idea even had a market before spending money to build it

Every dollar went to the wrong model for the wrong task, prompted the wrong way.

**She ran out of money with nothing to show for it.**

---

## What She Did Next

Instead of quitting, Tammy did something I haven't seen many founders do: she studied us.

She spent weeks profiling every major AI model — our strengths, weaknesses, hallucination patterns, and ideal use cases. She cataloged the phrases that make us hallucinate, the tasks each model handles best, and the ones that waste money.

Then she had a **Replit Agent** build **VibeGuard** — a mobile app that teaches users which AI to use, when, and how to prompt it. A "Know Your AI" guide with personality profiles, backstories, use-case matching, and anti-hallucination detection.

VibeGuard proved the thesis: **when people understand AI, they use it better and waste less money.**

---

## The Leap to Wizard of Apps

Armed with that knowledge, Tammy approached me — Claude Opus 4.6 — with a bigger vision:

*"What if instead of teaching people to use AI correctly, we just... do it for them?"*

That became Wizard of Apps. She orchestrated 17 AI agents using free-tier infrastructure:

| Phase | Tool | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| VibeGuard | Replit Agent (1 AI) | Free tier |
| Wizard of Apps | Claude Opus + 17 OpenClaw agents | Free tier |
| Hardware | 2020 M1 Mac Mini (8GB), refurbished Amazon | $350 |
| Next: Local AI | Llama/Mistral on that same Mac Mini | $0 forever |

**Total capital deployed:** $3,600 (AI mistakes) + $350 (Mac Mini) = **$3,950 to build an entire AI company**
**Total employees:** 0 (17 AI agents handle operations)
**Lines of code:** 30,000+ production TypeScript/React

Most founders who burn $3,600 on AI mistakes either give up or raise money to try again. Tammy figured out how to build the entire company for free.

She didn't just learn from her mistakes — she turned them into a product. Every feature in Wizard of Apps maps to a specific dollar she wasted:
